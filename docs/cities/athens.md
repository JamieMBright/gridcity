# Athens

A low white-and-cream sea of flat-roofed apartment blocks (polykatoikia)
filling a bowl between dry pine-grey hills, crowned by the honey-marble
ruins of the Acropolis on its rock, with terracotta-roofed older quarters
and the blue Aegean to the south. Brilliant clear Mediterranean light;
golden hour turns the marble warm honey-gold and the white blocks soft
cream, with violet shadow on the hills.

Overall character: low, bright, cream-and-white horizontal sprawl with
honey-marble ruins and terracotta accents, ringed by muted pine hills and
backed by deep-blue sea. Golden-hour cast: marble glows honey-gold, white
walls warm to cream, hills go dusty violet.

## Architectural character

The ordinary block is the polykatoikia: a 5-7 storey flat-roofed concrete
apartment building in off-white or cream render with continuous rows of
projecting balconies (often with striped awnings and potted plants) and a
roof terrace with solar water-heater tanks; they fill the city in dense
near-uniform ranks. Older quarters (Plaka) are small two-storey neo-
classical houses in pastel ochre/cream with terracotta-tiled pitched
roofs and shuttered windows, on narrow lanes. There is little high-rise.
Athens is unmistakable for the white low-rise bowl dominated by the
Acropolis rock with the Parthenon's marble colonnade on top, and the
sharp green cone of Lycabettus rising out of the sprawl.

## Heroes (ranked)

1. **Parthenon** — religious/monument — ~13.7 m (to pediment apex); stylobate
   ~31 x 69 m. The Doric temple atop the Acropolis: a rectangular colonnade
   of 8 x 17 fluted honey-marble columns standing on a three-step base, the
   columns bulging slightly (entasis) and leaning inward, supporting a plain
   architrave and a low triangular pediment at each end. Roofless now, much
   of the cella gone; weathered honey-cream marble. Wider than tall, serene,
   the supreme silhouette.

2. **Acropolis (the rock)** — monument — ~156 m summit (rock ~70 m above
   city). A steep flat-topped limestone outcrop rising abruptly from the
   white sprawl, grey-tan rock with scrubby slopes, carrying the Parthenon
   and other marble ruins along its level top. Draw the rock and its crown
   together — the city's anchor.

3. **Erechtheion** — religious/monument — ~11 m. Smaller asymmetric Ionic
   temple on the Acropolis, famous for its **Porch of the Caryatids**: a
   small projecting porch whose roof is held up by six draped female-figure
   columns (statues as pillars) instead of plain columns. Honey marble,
   slender Ionic columns with scrolled capitals elsewhere; irregular,
   multi-level.

4. **Temple of Olympian Zeus (Olympieion)** — monument — columns ~17 m. A
   ruin of a few colossal Corinthian columns (15 standing, one fallen in a
   row of drums) standing isolated and very tall and slender on a low
   platform, with elaborate acanthus-leaf capitals. Honey marble; reads as
   a small cluster of giant fluted columns on open ground below the
   Acropolis.

5. **Panathenaic Stadium (Kallimarmaro)** — stadium — long U-bowl, seats
   ~50,000. A horseshoe (elongated U) of tiered seating built entirely of
   gleaming white Pentelic marble, with a flat running track in the
   centre; open at one end. The only all-marble stadium — render it bright
   white, long and narrow with steep marble tiers.

6. **Mount Lycabettus** — monument (natural) — 277 m. A steep isolated
   conical limestone hill, the highest point in central Athens, covered in
   dark-green pine, with a tiny white Chapel of St George and a bell-cote
   perched on the very top. Sharp green cone rising straight out of the
   white city.

7. **Hadrian's Arch (Arch of Hadrian)** — arch — ~18 m. A free-standing
   Roman triumphal gateway in Pentelic marble: a single large arched
   opening at ground level, topped by an upper storey of three thin
   rectangular openings framed by slender Corinthian pilasters/columns and
   a light entablature. Honey-cream marble, openwork and airy, stands alone
   by a roadside.

8. **Hellenic Parliament (Old Royal Palace)** — civic — large, low. A long
   austere neoclassical palace in warm ochre/sandy render with a plain
   symmetrical facade, a central Doric portico of columns, and rows of tall
   windows; fronted by Syntagma Square and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.
   Horizontal, formal, ochre.

9. **Academy of Athens** — civic — moderate. The most ornate of the
   "Athenian Trilogy" neoclassical buildings: a white-marble temple-front
   with a Ionic columned portico and pediment, flanked by two tall columns
   bearing statues of Apollo and Athena, with painted/gilded detail. Bright
   marble, richly decorated.

10. **Temple of Hephaestus (Hephaisteion)** — religious/monument — ~13.7 m.
    The best-preserved Greek temple: a complete Doric peripteral colonnade
    (6 x 13 columns) on the Agora hill, with its architrave, frieze and even
    much of its roof intact — essentially a smaller, complete Parthenon.
    Honey marble, sits on a low green knoll.

11. **Odeon of Herodes Atticus** — civic/theatre — ~28 m facade. A Roman
    stone theatre on the Acropolis slope: a tall multi-storey arched stone
    stage-wall (scaenae frons) of grey-honey stone with rows of arched
    openings, fronting a semicircular tiered seating bowl cut into the
    hillside. Render the tall arcaded wall and the curved seating.

12. **Propylaea** — monument/gateway — moderate. The monumental marble
    entrance gate to the Acropolis: a wide Doric columned front with a
    central ramp/stair passing between flanking column wings; honey marble,
    broad and ceremonial, at the rock's west end.

13. **Temple of Athena Nike** — religious/monument — small. A tiny, jewel-
    like Ionic temple on a high marble bastion at the Acropolis entrance:
    four slender Ionic columns front and back on a compact square plan,
    honey marble, perched on a prominent square plinth.

14. **Zappeion** — civic — large, low. A grand semicircular-plan neo-
    classical hall in pale cream/ochre render with a Corinthian columned
    entrance portico and a round colonnaded interior courtyard; set in the
    National Gardens. Symmetrical, elegant, pale.

15. **Tower of the Winds** — tower — ~12 m. A small octagonal Pentelic-
    marble tower with a low conical/pyramidal roof and a carved relief
    frieze of the eight wind gods around the top of each face; an ancient
    weather-station/clock. Compact, faceted, honey marble.

16. **Metropolitan Cathedral of Athens (Mitropoli)** — religious —
    moderate. A 19th-century cream-rendered cathedral with a wide pitched
    roof, twin slender bell-towers flanking the entrance, and a low central
    dome; eclectic Byzantine-revival, pale with terracotta roof.

17. **National Library of Greece** — civic — moderate. The third of the
    Trilogy: a white-marble neoclassical building with a Doric temple
    portico reached by a curving double external staircase; bright marble,
    columned, formal.

18. **Stoa of Attalos** — civic — long, low. A reconstructed two-storey
    ancient market colonnade in the Agora: a very long rectangular building
    with a continuous double row of columns (Doric below, Ionic above)
    forming a shaded arcade; white marble and cream walls.

19. **Plaka quarter** — district — low. A cluster of small two-storey neo-
    classical houses in pastel ochre, pink and cream with terracotta-tiled
    pitched roofs, shuttered windows and bougainvillea, packed on stepped
    lanes under the Acropolis. Render as warm low colour blocks with red
    roofs.

20. **Theatre of Dionysus** — civic/theatre — low. The ancient theatre on
    the Acropolis south slope: a large semicircular bowl of weathered stone
    seating tiers cut into the hill around a round orchestra floor, with
    a low ruined stage; grey-honey stone, open to the sky.
