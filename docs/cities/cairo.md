# Cairo

A vast sand-and-ochre sea of flat-roofed brick apartment blocks, pierced
everywhere by slender minarets and ribbed stone domes, the Nile cutting
through green-fringed in the middle, and the Giza pyramids on the desert
edge. Dusty, hazy desert light; golden hour turns the whole city a warm
ochre-gold and the stone monuments honey-coloured.

Overall character: monochrome warm ochre-and-brick sprawl, accented by
pale-stone domes and minarets and one ribbon of green-blue river. Golden-
hour cast: everything goes amber-gold, monuments glow honey, deep blue
shadows in the canyon streets.

## Architectural character

The ordinary block is a 5-12 storey rectangular apartment building of
red brick on a concrete frame, often with bare unrendered or unfinished
brick upper floors, a flat roof loaded with water tanks, satellite dishes
and rebar stubs, and small balconies; they pack wall-to-wall in a dusty
beige sprawl. Historic Islamic Cairo is dense low stone: mosques and
madrasas with pale limestone walls, ribbed or carved stone domes, and
tall slender tiered minarets capped with little pavilions. The modern
centre (Downtown) has Belle-Epoque/Haussmann-style ochre apartment
blocks; a few glass towers line the Nile. Cairo is instantly known by the
forest of minarets over the brown sprawl and the pyramids on the skyline.

## Heroes (ranked)

1. **Great Pyramid of Giza** — monument — 138.5 m (orig. 146.6 m). A near-
   solid four-sided pyramid, faces pale honey limestone, weathered to a
   stepped texture; base:height roughly 1.57:1 (very broad-based). Missing
   smooth white casing except a few faint remnant patches near the apex,
   and the capstone is gone leaving a small flat top. Sits flat, massive,
   monumental on tawny desert sand. (Khafre and Menkaure pyramids beside
   it, slightly smaller; Khafre keeps a casing cap at its tip.)

2. **Great Sphinx of Giza** — monument — 20 m tall, 73 m long. A reclining
   lion body with a human (pharaoh) head wearing a striped nemes headdress,
   carved from honey-grey limestone bedrock, weathered and pitted, nose
   broken off. Low, long, couchant, facing east in front of the pyramids.

3. **Mosque of Muhammad Ali (Alabaster Mosque)** — religious — ~52 m
   (domes); 84 m minarets. Ottoman silhouette on the Citadel hilltop: a
   large central pale-alabaster dome surrounded by four half-domes and
   small corner domes, flanked by two extremely tall, thin, pencil-pointed
   minarets. Pale grey-cream stone; dominant on the skyline above the city.

4. **Cairo Tower** — tower — 187 m. A free-standing concrete tower: a
   tall slender tapering shaft sheathed in a lattice of crisscrossing
   cast-concrete lozenge openwork (modelled on a latticed lotus stalk),
   flaring at the top into a circular cantilevered observation crown/
   "lotus blossom" with a saucer deck. Sandy buff concrete, on Gezira
   Island by the Nile.

5. **Citadel of Cairo (Saladin)** — civic/fort — large, hilltop. A massive
   medieval fortress of pale-honey stone curtain walls with round and
   square towers and crenellations, wrapping a promontory of the Mokattam
   hills; the Muhammad Ali Mosque rises inside it. Read as a long fortified
   stone wall-and-tower line on high ground.

6. **Mosque-Madrasa of Sultan Hasan** — religious — huge, ~57 m walls; 81 m
   minaret. Colossal Mamluk monument: towering sheer pale-stone walls with
   a deeply recessed, very tall arched portal, a large ribbed stone dome at
   the rear, and one immense tall tiered minaret. Severe, fortress-like,
   honey limestone.

7. **Al-Azhar Mosque** — religious — low sprawling; multiple minarets. The
   1,000-year Fatimid mosque: a wide low complex around an open arcaded
   courtyard, with several differing tall slender minarets (some twin-
   finialled) of pale carved stone rising over the rooftops. "City of a
   thousand minarets" in miniature.

8. **Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)** — civic — low, vast. Modern: a long low
   wedge-shaped building whose translucent alabaster-stone front facade is
   a huge folded triangular screen (a stylised pyramid/Sierpinski pattern)
   facing the Giza pyramids; pale stone and glass, very horizontal.

9. **Khan el-Khalili** — district — low. A dense warren of narrow stone-
   arched alleys and small shopfronts under awnings, with carved wooden
   mashrabiya screens and ornate Mamluk gateways; render as a tight cluster
   of low ochre buildings with arched openings and hanging lamps/canopies.

10. **Bab Zuwayla** — arch/gate — ~25 m with minarets. A massive medieval
    stone city gate: two large rounded stone bastion-towers flanking a tall
    arched gateway, with two slender minarets (from the adjacent mosque)
    rising directly off the tops of the towers. Honey stone, fortified.

11. **Cairo Opera House** — civic — low, modern. A low geometric Islamic-
    modern complex on Gezira Island: clean white/pale-stone cubic volumes
    with arched and lattice motifs and a low dome; set in gardens by the
    Nile.

12. **Egyptian Museum (Tahrir)** — civic — low, grand. A 1901 neo-
    classical/neo-Pharaonic building in salmon-terracotta pink render with
    a domed central entrance, arched windows and a symmetrical columned
    facade; faces Tahrir Square. Distinct dusty-pink colour.

13. **Hanging Church (Coptic Cairo)** — religious — small. A Coptic
    Orthodox church built atop a Roman gate so it appears suspended: a pale
    facade with twin small bell-towers topped by little crosses and a
    wooden pitched roof, reached by a stair; among the old fortress walls.

14. **Al-Rifa'i Mosque** — religious — large, ~58 m walls; 90 m minarets.
    The huge royal mosque facing Sultan Hasan, built 1869-1912 in matching
    Mamluk-revival style: massive honey-stone walls, a tall arched portal,
    a ribbed dome and tall slender minarets — a near-twin to its neighbour.

15. **Ibn Tulun Mosque** — religious — low, vast. The oldest intact mosque
    in Cairo: a very large low square enclosure of plain sand-coloured
    brick/stucco with a wide open courtyard, and a unique minaret with an
    external helical (spiral) staircase wrapping its tapering tower.

16. **Iconic Tower (New Capital CBD)** — skyscraper — 394 m. Tallest in
    Africa, 77 floors. A tapering rounded-rectangular shaft that narrows
    as it rises and is sheathed in gold-bronze tinted glass with a faceted
    crown; an obelisk-inspired form. (In the new desert capital east of
    Cairo, amid a row of shorter glass towers.)

17. **Qaitbay complex / City of the Dead domes** — religious — low cluster.
    The Mamluk necropolis: a field of low mausolea each topped by a tall
    carved-stone dome with intricate geometric/floral relief, and slender
    minarets — a skyline of pale ribbed domes over the desert edge.

18. **Nilometer (Rhoda Island)** — tower/civic — small. A conical-roofed
    pavilion over a deep square stone well with a central graduated marble
    column; pointed pavilion roof on slim columns by the river.

19. **Abdeen Palace** — civic — low, grand. A long symmetrical 19th-century
    European-style royal palace in pale cream render with rows of arched
    windows, balustrades and a central pedimented entrance; ornate, formal,
    horizontal.

20. **Cairo Railway Station (Ramses)** — station — low, long. A grand 1892
    terminus blending neo-Mamluk and European styles: a long pale facade
    with horseshoe arches, a central arched hall and decorative parapet,
    fronting Ramses Square.
