# Pune

A dense, low-and-mid-rise sprawl of concrete-frame buildings in ochre and
cream cement render, packed across a flat plateau ringed by brown-green
hills, with the muddy Mula-Mutha river threading through. Old Maratha
peths hold black-basalt fort walls, teak gateways and brightly polychromed
temples; the east and west edges erupt into mirror-glass IT-park towers
(Hinjawadi, Kharadi). The light is hot and hazy — golden hour turns the
render saffron-gold, the basalt warm charcoal, and the haze pinks the
distant Sahyadri hills. Red Mangalore-tile roofs and saffron temple
finials punctuate the grey-cream mass.

Overall character: a warm grey-and-cream concrete plateau-city, neither
white nor red but dusty-ochre, lit hazy-gold; the eye is caught by pops of
saffron-and-gold temples, near-black basalt fort stone, dark teak
woodwork, and red tile roofs scattered through the render — then by cool
glass towers on the IT fringes. Monsoon greens the hills; dry season turns
them khaki. Everything reads sun-bleached and a little hazy.

## Architectural character

Ordinary Pune is dense low-to-mid-rise (3-12 storey) reinforced-concrete
construction finished in cement plaster painted cream, buff or muted
ochre, with flat RCC roofs cluttered with black water tanks and dishes;
the old central peths layer this over surviving wadas — Maratha courtyard
mansions of brick-on-stone plinths with deep teak gateways, carved
balconies and red Mangalore-tiled verandah roofs. The IT-park CBDs at
Hinjawadi (west) and Kharadi/Magarpatta (east) stand apart as clusters of
boxy blue-glass curtain-wall towers, the only real verticality. Heritage
is twofold: black Deccan-basalt military stone (Shaniwar Wada ramparts,
the Sahyadri hill-forts, the rock-cut Pataleshwar caves) and vividly
polychromed Hindu temples crowned with gold-finialed shikharas and dressed
in saffron and vermilion. Pune is recognisable by this collision — a hazy
ochre concrete sprawl on a flat plateau, studded with black fort stone and
gold-and-saffron temples, ringed by brown-green flat-topped hills, with
mirror-glass tech towers on the edges and the brown river looping through.

## Heroes (ranked)

1. **Shaniwar Wada** — Maratha fort-palace — ~6.4 m granite perimeter
   walls (original 7-storey palace interior, burnt 1828; not verified in
   m). A large fortified rectangle of heavy dark basalt-granite ramparts
   with nine bastion towers and five gateways enclosing a now-open garden
   on the original foundations. Massing reads as low thick stone walls
   (not a tall building) — warm charcoal-grey stone, square crenellated
   bastions at the corners. The interior palace is gone; show ruined
   plinths and lawns inside the ring. Long, broad, fortress-flat.

2. **Shaniwar Wada — Delhi Darwaza (main gate)** — fortified gateway —
   ~6 m+ (elephant-height doors; exact height unverified). The signature
   feature: a tall rectangular dark-stone gatehouse flanked by two square
   bastions, with massive twin teak doors studded with rows of sharp steel
   spikes (a 9×8 grid at elephant-forehead height). Pointed-arch recess
   over the doors, machicolation slots in the bastions. Dark teak (warm
   brown) doors set in near-black basalt; the most-photographed silhouette
   in Pune. Render it as the hero face of the wada.

3. **Sinhagad Fort** — hill fort — at 1,317 m AMSL (≈760 m above the
   plain). A long irregular ring of dark basalt rampart walls following
   the brow of an isolated flat-topped Sahyadri cliff, with two stone
   gateways (Pune & Kalyan darwaja). Reads as a dark stone crown on a
   steep khaki-green hill, walls hugging the contour, sheer cliff faces
   below. A slender modern TV relay mast breaks the skyline. Massing is
   horizontal stonework on a dramatic height, not a built tower.

4. **Aga Khan Palace** — palace — 3 storeys; built-up ~6.9 ha within a
   19-acre garden (no m height verified). Indo-Saracenic: a broad,
   low-spreading two/three-storey palace ringed on all sides by an open
   arcaded corridor 2.5 m deep — rows of pointed/cusped arches on slender
   columns. Topped with pediments and small turrets. Walls pale cream/buff
   render; arches in repeating rhythm; set in expansive green lawns. Reads
   as a wide, elegant arcaded block, very horizontal, garden-surrounded.
   Three small white marble samadhi pavilions in a walled corner.

5. **Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple** — temple — idol 2.2 m tall, 1 m
   wide, ~40 kg gold (temple tower height not verified). A compact urban
   temple with a tall ornate shikhara/gopuram-style facade, brightly
   polychromed (saffron, gold, red, white) and heavily carved. The deep
   interior frames the famous seated Ganesh idol — gilded, garlanded,
   under a decorated canopy, glowing gold. Crowded street frontage,
   festoons and marigold garlands. Render gold-and-saffron, ornate, vivid
   against the grey peth around it.

6. **Parvati Hill temple complex** — hilltop temple — hill 640 m AMSL,
   108 stone steps to the top (temple height not verified). A small
   cluster of black-stone Peshwa-era temples with modest shikhara towers
   on the flat summit of a green hillock, reached by a long straight
   flight of stone steps up the slope. Dark basalt walls, small gold
   finials, white-edged trim. Reads as a little dark-and-gold temple
   crown on a rounded green/khaki hill rising over the city.

7. **Pataleshwar Cave Temple** — rock-cut temple — open court ~95×90 ft;
   sanctums ~39 ft long, 27.5 ft deep (8th c. Rashtrakuta). A monolithic
   excavation carved DOWN into a low basalt outcrop, not a built-up
   structure: a sunken square court fronting a pillared hall of squat
   square dark-basalt pillars (eight-pillar facade, five rows deep). In
   the court stands a freestanding circular Nandi-mandapa — a low ring of
   stone pillars sheltering the bull, on a carved plinth. All near-black
   basalt, blocky, austere, partly weathered; set in a green garden.
   Render as a dark stone pit-temple at ground level.

8. **Pune Junction railway station** — station — 6 platforms (no height
   verified). A long, low colonial-era terminus building: cream/ochre
   rendered masonry, a horizontal multi-bay frontage with repeated
   arched/rectangular openings, pitched red-tile and flat roofs, a central
   entrance block slightly raised, signage band along the facade. Reads as
   a wide, low, busy transport shed-and-frontage; trainsheds and platform
   canopies behind. Horizontal, utilitarian, warm-rendered.

9. **Vishrambaug Wada** — Maratha mansion — ~20,000 sq ft, 2 storeys (no
   m height). A broad two-storey peth mansion famous for its façade: a
   grand central teak entrance and a long first-floor balcony of
   intricately carved dark teak woodwork running across cream/ochre
   rendered walls, under a red Mangalore-tile pitched roof. Reads as a
   wide low ornate townhouse — cream wall, dark carved-wood balcony band,
   red roof. The carved teak balcony is its signature.

10. **Lal Mahal** — palace (modern reconstruction) — 2 storeys (no m
    height; rebuilt 1980s). A medium red-toned masonry palace block
    (Shivaji's childhood home, reconstructed): warm red/terracotta walls,
    arched and bracketed windows, modest crenellation, a small central
    entrance — squarish, two-storey, civic-scale. Reads distinctly RED
    against the cream city (its name means "Red Palace"); flat-ish roofline
    with small corner detailing.

11. **Hinjawadi IT towers (Rajiv Gandhi Infotech Park)** — office towers —
    typically ~10-20 storeys (no single verified m). A loose cluster of
    boxy, flat-topped modern office blocks clad in blue-grey reflective
    curtain-wall glass and grey spandrel bands, set among wide roads,
    parking and palm-dotted campus lawns. Rectangular slabs and stepped
    boxes, corporate and repetitive. The west-edge skyline counterpoint to
    the old stone city — cool glass against warm render. Render several
    grouped towers of varying height.

12. **Kharadi / EON IT-park towers** — office towers — ~10-20 storeys (no
    verified m). Same boxy blue-glass curtain-wall vocabulary as Hinjawadi
    but on the eastern river corridor, on the banks of the Mula-Mutha:
    grouped mirror-glass office slabs with grey frames, podium parking,
    landscaped plazas. Mirror the IT-park treatment on the opposite edge
    of the map for east-west symmetry.

13. **Shinde Chhatri** — memorial (cenotaph) — 2-storey hall + temple
    spire (no m verified). An ornate Rajasthani-style memorial: a richly
    carved hall of yellow sandstone over a black-basalt base/sanctum, with
    a Shiva-temple shikhara of finely carved yellow stone rising above,
    studded with saint figures, and English-style coloured-glass windows.
    Reads as two-tone — black stone lower, golden-yellow carved stone upper
    and spire — small, jewel-like, intensely detailed.

14. **Ohel David Synagogue (Laal Deval)** — religious — 90 ft (≈27 m)
    clock tower. English Gothic-revival: a brilliant RED brick + Deccan-
    trap-stone building with pointed-arch windows, capped by a tall square
    red tower with a clock face on each side and a steeply pitched grey
    slate roof. Reads vividly red ("Laal Deval" = Red Temple) with a
    prominent clock spire — the standout colonial landmark. (Note: Pune's
    Sassoon-funded heritage landmark; the David Sassoon *Library* is in
    Mumbai, not Pune — substituted here as the true Pune equivalent.)

15. **Mahatma Phule Mandai (market)** — civic market — 80 ft (≈24 m)
    central tower. A colonial grey-stone market: a squat octagonal central
    clock tower 80 ft high, ringed by a tiled-roof verandah, with eight
    (built: several) market wings radiating outward, each entered through
    pointed Gothic arches. Masonry of local grey basalt-stone, red-tile
    roofs over the wings. Reads as a low spoked star with a stubby central
    octagon tower — busy, civic, grey-and-red.

16. **Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park (Katraj)** — civic / zoo — 130 acres
    incl. 42-acre Katraj Lake (no building height). Not a building: render
    as a large green wooded enclosure with a broad lake, low animal-
    enclosure structures, perimeter fencing and tree canopy, set against
    the brown Katraj hills on the south edge. Green-and-water landmark
    mass rather than a structure.

17. **Sarasbaug (Talyatla Ganpati)** — temple-in-garden — small temple,
    25-acre garden (no m height). A small white-and-saffron Ganesh temple
    ("Ganpati of the lake") sitting on what was an island in a now-dried
    artificial lake, set in a large green public garden. Reads as a modest
    bright temple with a small shikhara at the centre of an open green
    park — white walls, saffron/gold trim, surrounded by lawns and trees.

18. **College of Engineering Pune (COEP) main building** — civic
    (institutional) — colonial, low-rise (no m verified). A long, dignified
    colonial-Gothic teaching building in cream/buff render and grey stone
    dressings, arched windows and a central gabled/towered entrance, with a
    riverside frontage onto the Mula-Mutha (its rowing/boat club sits on
    the water below). Reads as a wide, low, symmetrical institutional block
    by the river — heritage, restrained, cream-and-grey.

19. **COEP Boat Club (riverside)** — civic / waterfront — small low
    structure. A modest low boathouse and jetty on the muddy Mula-Mutha
    below COEP, with rowing shells, a pitched-roof clubhouse and a stepped
    riverbank. Reads as a small waterfront detail — low building, boats,
    brown water, green bank — anchoring the river through the city centre.

20. **Bund Garden / riverfront** — civic / waterfront — riverside park &
    weir (no structure height). A landscaped green riverside promenade
    along the Mula-Mutha with a low weir/bund across the brown water, tree
    avenues, ghats/steps to the river and walking paths. Render as a green
    edge with a low water-control bund — gives the muddy river a designed
    civic moment in the city core.
