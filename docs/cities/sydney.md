Sydney's overall colour character is a sun-bleached pairing of honey sandstone, terracotta-red roofs and silvery eucalypt green against an intensely bright harbour blue; the very high-UV southern-hemisphere light keeps daytime contrast hard and shadows crisp-navy, while golden hour throws a strong warm amber wash that makes the sandstone glow and the western glass faces flare orange.

## Architectural character

Sydney's residential fabric is overwhelmingly low and pitched: Federation red-brick bungalows under burnt-orange terracotta hip-and-gable roofs, single-storey weatherboard cottages, and dense rows of Victorian terraces fronted by two-storey cast-iron "lacework" verandahs in the inner east and Paddington. Among them sit Art Deco walk-up flats and, increasingly, slab-and-glass apartment blocks along the harbour. The CBD is a tight cluster of cool blue-glass towers crowding around a handful of preserved honey-coloured sandstone monuments — the Italian-Renaissance GPO, the Romanesque QVB and the French Second-Empire Town Hall — with the slender gold-lollipop Sydney Tower spiking above everything. Roof form splits cleanly: suburban Sydney is a sea of steep terracotta pitches and grey corrugated iron, while the CBD reads as flat-topped glass prisms. What makes Sydney instantly itself, though, is the water and two objects on it: the white shell "sails" of the Opera House and the steel "Coathanger" arch of the Harbour Bridge, set against a bright blue harbour ringed by silvery eucalypt headlands.

## Heroes (ranked)

1. **Sydney Opera House**
   - Type: opera-house / civic
   - Height: ~67 m (highest roof shell)
   - Recreate-it spec: A cluster of white precast-concrete shell "sails" sitting on a broad, low sandstone-coloured granite podium; two main rows of three nested vaults each, rising to sharp points and leaning back, plus a smaller detached restaurant shell. Surfaces are off-white with a faint cream sheen from glossy chevron tiles, picking up warm gold at dusk; broad and low, roughly 2:1 width:height. Each sail is a segment of a sphere — fat-bottomed, tapering to a curved spine — fronted by amber-glass curtain walls under the shell mouths.

2. **Sydney Harbour Bridge**
   - Type: bridge / arch
   - Height: 134 m (top of arch above water)
   - Recreate-it spec: A single huge through-arch of battleship-grey latticed steel — the "Coathanger" — a fat parabolic bow above a flat roadway deck slung beneath it on vertical hangers. At each of the four corners stands a squat sandstone-faced granite pylon (decorative, pale honey-grey) framing the arch ends. Very wide and relatively shallow: span hugely exceeds rise, roughly 4:1 width:height; the steelwork should read as a dense criss-cross truss, darker grey in shadow.

3. **Sydney Tower (Centrepoint / Eye)**
   - Type: tower
   - Height: ~305-309 m (Wikidata 306 m; commonly cited 309 m to tip)
   - Recreate-it spec: A very slender golden "lollipop" — a thin shaft of bundled cables and a plain column topped by a fat gold-amber turret (the observation/restaurant pod and water tank) with a spike above. The shaft is pale grey-gold, the crown disk is burnished bronze-gold catching sun; extremely tall and thin, roughly 1:12 width:height, standing well clear above the surrounding glass boxes.

4. **Queen Victoria Building (QVB)**
   - Type: civic / dome
   - Height: ~30 m wide x 190 m long block; central dome dominant
   - Recreate-it spec: A long honey-sandstone Romanesque palazzo filling a whole block, arcaded ground floor with round-arched windows, crowned by a large central copper dome gone pale verdigris green flanked by smaller domes and turrets. Warm tan stone with paired arched windows; long and low with the green dome as the single tall accent.

5. **Sydney Harbour Bridge pylons ("the Coathanger" pylons)**
   - Type: monument / arch
   - Height: ~89 m
   - Recreate-it spec: Four matching square towers of pale honey sandstone-faced concrete, Art Deco massing — tapering slightly, with a stepped flat top and vertical fluting. They flank the bridge deck in pairs; read them as solid, blockish, warm-grey stone monoliths roughly 1:3 width:height. (Drawn as standalone gate-markers if the bridge arch itself isn't shown.)

6. **St Mary's Cathedral**
   - Type: religious
   - Height: 74.6 m (spires)
   - Recreate-it spec: A long Gothic Revival cathedral in honey-to-grey Sydney sandstone, with twin pointed spires at the south front and a tall central crossing area. Steep pitched roofs, pinnacles and pointed-arch windows; the stone is warm tan ageing to grey-green. Tall narrow proportions at the spired end, roughly 1:2 footprint, with a strong vertical pull from the matched spires.

7. **Sydney Town Hall**
   - Type: civic
   - Height: ~2 main storeys plus a tall clock tower
   - Recreate-it spec: An ornate honey-sandstone French Second-Empire pile with a steep grey mansard roof, heavily carved facade, and a tall projecting clock tower with a domed/pavilion top. Wedding-cake richness in pale tan stone; the clock tower is the silhouette anchor, roughly twice the height of the main block.

8. **Central railway station**
   - Type: station
   - Height: ~75 m clock tower
   - Recreate-it spec: A long sandstone terminus in warm honey ashlar with arched windows and a colonnade, dominated by a tall square Italianate clock tower with a clock face on each side and a small pyramidal/domed cap. Broad horizontal block, one slender tall tower; pale tan stone, grey roof.

9. **Anzac Memorial (Hyde Park)**
   - Type: monument
   - Height: ~30 m
   - Recreate-it spec: A compact Art Deco ziggurat of rose-pink/buff granite — a stepped cubic mass rising to a faceted, stepped pyramidal dome, with tall thin vertical buttress fins and seated figural relief sculptures at the corners. Squat and symmetrical, roughly 1:1, in warm pink-grey stone, mirrored in a long reflecting pool.

10. **Crown Sydney (Barangaroo)**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 271.3 m (75 floors)
    - Recreate-it spec: A tall twisting trio of glass "petals" — three curved fluted lobes spiralling upward and tapering, like a soft three-bladed propeller stretched vertical. All glass, cool blue-silver with bronze mullions; very slender and sculptural, roughly 1:7 width:height, with a rounded, lobed crown rather than a flat top — the tallest thing on the western harbour.

11. **Chifley Tower**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 241 m (53 storeys)
    - Recreate-it spec: A pale stepped 1990s tower with a rounded/curved corner facade and a distinctive crown — a tall thin antenna-mast rising from a tiered, faceted top. Stone-and-glass cladding in cool grey with blue glazing; slender, roughly 1:6, the spire making it read taller than its neighbours.

12. **25 Martin Place (MLC Centre)**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 228 m (67 storeys)
    - Recreate-it spec: A pure white octagonal concrete tower by Harry Seidler — eight flat faces, sharp vertical fins running its full height, no setbacks, flat top. Off-white precast concrete, no glass curtain wall feel; a clean tall faceted column, roughly 1:7, reading bone-white against blue-glass neighbours.

13. **World Tower**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 230 m (residential)
    - Recreate-it spec: A very tall slim residential tower with a curved, tapering glass crown and stacked balcony banding. Blue-grey glass and pale spandrels, a rounded sail-like top; extremely slender, roughly 1:9, the tallest pin in the southern CBD.

14. **Governor Phillip Tower**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 227 m roof (254 m to mast tip)
    - Recreate-it spec: A tall slab on a massive 10-storey stone base, with a flat top crowned by a striking open "comb" of nine tall thin vertical blade-fins projecting above the roofline. Cool grey stone and glass; a broad-faced slender block, roughly 1:5, instantly known by the spiky finned crown.

15. **Aurora Place**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: 218 m (41 storeys)
    - Recreate-it spec: A curved all-glass Renzo Piano tower with a softly bowed face and white-framed glass "sail" fins peeling up past the roof at one corner, echoing the Opera House. Pale crystalline blue-white glass, lightweight and translucent; slender with a wind-blown curved top, roughly 1:5.

16. **Australia Square**
    - Type: skyscraper
    - Height: ~170 m (cylindrical)
    - Recreate-it spec: A plain circular cylinder tower (Harry Seidler) — a smooth round shaft of off-white lightweight concrete with continuous fine vertical ribs, flat top, set in an open plaza. Render it as a true cylinder, pale grey-white, roughly 1:6, distinct for having no corners at all.

17. **Customs House**
    - Type: civic
    - Height: ~4-5 storeys
    - Recreate-it spec: A dignified honey-sandstone Classical block at Circular Quay with a colonnaded/pilastered facade, a central clock and pediment, and a rusticated arched base. Warm tan stone, symmetrical and horizontal, low and broad with a modest central crown.

18. **General Post Office (GPO)**
    - Type: civic
    - Height: ~3 storeys plus clock tower; facade 114 m long
    - Recreate-it spec: A very long Victorian Italian-Renaissance sandstone palazzo, two storeys of arcaded colonnades in pale Pyrmont sandstone, topped by a tall slender clock-and-bell tower. Honey-tan stone, strongly horizontal arcades, one tall tower as the accent.

19. **Luna Park (face entrance)**
    - Type: amusement
    - Height: ~9 m grinning-face arch; flanking towers ~38 m
    - Recreate-it spec: A giant smiling clown/Moon face as a walk-through entrance arch — wide grinning mouth as the doorway, big round eyes, flanked by two tall slender Art Deco towers. Cream face with bright red lips, yellow rays, painted candy colours; cartoonish and small in footprint but loud, sitting right under the bridge.

20. **Fort Denison**
    - Type: monument
    - Height: small island; round Martello tower ~2-3 storeys
    - Recreate-it spec: A tiny rocky harbour islet topped by a squat circular sandstone Martello tower with a flat castellated top and a thin flagpole. Honey-grey rough stone drum on bare rock, very small scale, ringed by bright blue water — a lone stubby cylinder rising straight from the harbour.
