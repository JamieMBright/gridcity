## Description
skyscaper in New York City

One Manhattan Square is a luxury residential skyscraper building developed by Extell Development Company in the Two Bridges neighborhood of Manhattan, New York City. Built from 2014 to 2019, the skyscraper was built on the site of a former Pathmark grocery store, which was demolished in 2014. The building topped out in 2017 and stands out significantly within the context of the neighborhood, at 847 feet (258 m) in height, the next highest structure being the Manhattan Bridge at roughly 336 feet (102 m) in height. The building topped out in September 2017 and was complete by August 2019. A 13-story affordable housing component is located separately on-site.
