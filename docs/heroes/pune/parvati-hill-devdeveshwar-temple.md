## Description
Pune's holy hill, the oldest surviving Peshwa-era structure in the city, reached by a famous flight of 103 wide black-stone steps. Nana Saheb Peshwa built the Devdeveshwar temple in c.1749 in fulfilment of a vow after his mother Kashibai was cured. It is a Shiv-panchayatan: a central black-basalt Shiva temple ringed by four smaller subsidiary shrines to Surya, Ganesha, Vishnu and Bhavani, each crowned by copper kalashas. Chalukyan-style dwarpala guardian sculptures flank the entrance. The summit also holds the Peshwa Museum and commands a panorama over the whole city.
