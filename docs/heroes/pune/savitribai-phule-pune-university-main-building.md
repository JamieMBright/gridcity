## Description
Originally the monsoon residence of the Governors of Bombay, built in 1864 in Ganeshkhind to a design by James Trubshawe, said to be inspired by Osborne House. Built of basalt with Gothic arches, balustrades, gargoyles and pointed towers, its signature feature is a tall Italianate bell-tower (campanile). Now the administrative heart of the 'Oxford of the East' campus.
