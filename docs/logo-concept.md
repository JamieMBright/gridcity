# ElectriCity — logo concept note

A blank-concept redesign of the brand mark. The old mark (glowing "ELECTRI" +
a literal lightning bolt + "CITY" as blocky buildings) was the *template*
answer for an electricity game: the bolt = electricity cliché plus a
city-builder skyline. But the player here is not building a city — they are
the **electricity network operator**. The mark should belong to the
operator's world (nodes, lines, substations, the dusk-glow of a powered
area), and survive at 16px.

Design lenses used:
- **color-theory** — the sunset ramp (orange → gold) is the *energized*
  signal; the deep navy + cool slate is the *dark grid*. One warm focal
  point carries the hierarchy, and it survives a grayscale test (the lit
  core still dominates). Restraint: two hue families, no more.
- **frontend-design** — spend boldness in one place (the glowing node);
  keep everything else quiet. Don't reach for the default electricity icon
  (bolt). Ground the mark in the subject's vernacular.
- **canvas-design** — the niche reference woven in is grid *topology*
  itself: a busbar node where transmission lines converge. Those who know
  the industry feel it; everyone else just reads a glowing hub.

## Concepts explored

### A — "The Node"  ★ chosen
A substation node where transmission lines converge, lit warm against the
dark network. The operator's actual instrument. **Why it fits:** it *is* the
network, not a symbol of electricity; the warm-lit core is the "powering an
area makes it glow" hook, built in; the cool radiating lines recede so the
warm node reads first (correct contrast hierarchy). **Colour:** golden-hour
gradient core (`#ffd27a → #ff9a2e → #f47714`) on `#0d1228`, slate-blue lines
(`#6f7ba6`) and navy outer nodes (`#39446e`). **16px verdict:** survives — a
glowing core on a dark rounded tile with a faint X of grid lines.

### B — "The Window"
A city-block silhouette cut from the navy with one warm-lit window — the
literal glow hook, quiet and human. **Why it half-fits:** lovely idea, but
the single lit window is a speck at 16px (fails the killer test) and the
blocks read as a stats dashboard, not a grid. Rejected on legibility.

### C — "The Spark E"
A monogram **E** built from grid topology: three horizontal busbars + a
riser, nodes at the junctions, energized warm at the top fading to cool navy
at the bottom (the unpowered frontier). **Why it half-fits:** elegant and
on-theme, but a pure letterform monogram drifts back toward the wordmark
direction the brief asked me to move past, and at 16px the E collapses to an
ambiguous blob. Rejected.

### D — "The Pylon Node"
A refined transmission tower reduced to its lattice trapezoid, glow at its
base, lightning-bolt cliché dropped. **Why it half-fits:** handsome, but
it's still *iterating the existing pylon mark* (brief: start blank), and the
thin lattice lines smear to mud at 16px. Rejected.

## Decision

**Concept A "The Node."** It is the freshest direction, the truest to a
network-operator game, it bakes in the dusk-glow hook, and it is the only
mark that stays legible at favicon size.

## The wordmark + lockup

"Electri" (off-white, energized) → "City" (gold/orange, the operator's warm
grid) set in a heavy geometric sans with tight tracking, read as **one**
word via the colour shift. The signature detail: the dot of the **"i" is the
glowing grid-node**, so the wordmark quotes the mark. A small-caps tagline,
**LONDON GRID OPERATOR**, grounds the identity (structural label that encodes
something true, not decoration). The lockup is code-drawn SVG; the raster
PNGs (favicon / app-icon / wordmark) are generated from the SVG sources via
`tools/genIcons.mjs` so they never drift.
